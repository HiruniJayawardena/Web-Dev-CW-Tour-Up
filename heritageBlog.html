Heritage Places in Sri Lanka

With a 3000 year history, Sri Lanka is home to some of the world's most ancient cities, such as Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa, and Digamadulla. These cities' once magnificent townships, palaces, temples, monasteries, hospitals, and theaters, all of which were intricately carved and modeled out of stone, have since been abandoned and forgotten amidst the country's towering jungles.

The Kingdom of Anuradhapura is the most renowned and beautiful of all the ancient cities in Lanka. One of the oldest continuously inhabited towns in the world and Sri Lanka's third and longest-serving capital is also one of the most revered by Buddhists worldwide.It served as Sri Lanka's capital from the Fourth Century BC until the turn of the eleventh Century and was one of South Asia's most enduring and stable hubs of political authority and urban activity. During the Anuradhapura era, Digamadulla, the Eastern kingdom of Sri Lanka, served as the nation's agricultural and spiritual center. The Kingdom of Kandy, the final kingdom in Sri Lanka, is proof of the country's people's capacity to emerge from the ashes.

Polonnaruwa



Polonnaruwa is an ancient city in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka that was once the capital of the country from the 11th to the 13th century. The city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is renowned for its well-preserved ancient ruins, temples, and royal palace complex. Polonnaruwa was once a thriving city and the center of a powerful kingdom, and its ruins provide a fascinating glimpse into the grandeur and sophistication of ancient Sri Lankan civilization. The city's many attractions include the Royal Palace complex, the Gal Vihara rock temple with its stunning statues of Buddha, and the ancient Parakrama Samudra reservoir, which was once used for irrigation and is still in use today. Visitors to Polonnaruwa can explore the ancient city by foot or bicycle, and experience the unique history and culture of Sri Lanka.

Anuradhapura



Anuradhapura is an ancient city in Sri Lanka, located in the North Central Province. It was once the capital of Sri Lanka from the 4th century BC until the 11th century AD and is considered one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. The city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is renowned for its well-preserved ancient ruins, temples, and stupas. Anuradhapura was once a center of Buddhist culture and learning and is home to some of the most important Buddhist sites in Sri Lanka, including the Sri Maha Bodhi, a sacred fig tree believed to be the oldest recorded tree in the world, and the Ruwanwelisaya stupa, one of the largest stupas in Sri Lanka. Visitors to Anuradhapura can explore the ancient city by foot or bicycle, and experience the unique history and culture of Sri Lanka.

Dambulla



The Dambulla Cave Temple, also known as the Golden Temple of Dambulla, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the central Matale District of Sri Lanka. It is a complex of cave temples featuring ancient Buddhist murals, sculptures, and architecture, dating back to the 1st century BC. The cave temple complex includes five main caves, each adorned with hundreds of Buddha statues and intricate paintings depicting scenes from the life of Buddha. The temple is renowned for its colorful murals, which cover an area of over 2,100 square meters, and for its towering golden Buddha statue, which is the largest in Sri Lanka. Visitors to the Dambulla Cave Temple can enjoy a breathtaking panoramic view of the surrounding countryside from the top of the temple complex, as well as explore the nearby local markets and experience the unique cultural traditions of Sri Lanka.

Sigiriya



Sigiriya is an ancient fortress and palace complex located in the central Matale District of Sri Lanka. Built in the 5th century AD by King Kasyapa, Sigiriya is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the most remarkable archaeological sites in the world. The complex includes the remains of a palace, gardens, and a moat, as well as several frescoes and ancient graffiti. The most striking feature of Sigiriya is the massive rock outcrop rising 200 meters above the surrounding plain, which was once the site of the king's palace. Visitors can climb the rock via a stairway and see the remains of the palace and the beautiful views of the surrounding countryside. Sigiriya is also renowned for its beautiful water gardens and the Mirror Wall, a highly polished wall that reflects the frescoes and inscriptions on it. Overall, Sigiriya is a fascinating glimpse into the rich history and culture of Sri Lanka.

Yapahuwa



Yapahuwa is an ancient fortress and capital city located in the northwestern part of Sri Lanka. It was built in the 13th century by King Buvanekabahu I and served as the capital of Sri Lanka for a brief period before being abandoned. Yapahuwa is known for its stunning rock fortress, which rises over 300 feet above the surrounding plains and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. The fortress includes a series of staircases, rock carvings, and ancient ruins, including the remains of a palace, temple, and library. Visitors to Yapahuwa can explore the ancient ruins and learn about the rich history and culture of Sri Lanka. Yapahuwa is also home to several natural attractions, including a picturesque waterfall and a nature reserve with a diverse range of flora and fauna. Overall, Yapahuwa is a fascinating glimpse into the ancient history and culture of Sri Lanka.
